## 2026-06-03 — Family Tree Research With AI

Post: https://alanhalley.com/writing/family-tree-ai/

### LinkedIn — posted
> My family name in Missouri was always pronounced "Holly." Not "Halley." My father's generation changed it — probably in high school, or the Army. I only confirmed this last week.
>
> I was researching my family tree. FindaGrave blocks bots. There is no API. What works is simpler: open a memorial page, copy the family section, paste it into an AI conversation, ask what it means.
>
> The AI holds every date and location you hand it simultaneously. Paste that a woman was born in Virginia in 1786 and buried in Missouri in 1856, that her son was born in Kentucky in 1820. The AI places the migration without being asked.
>
> I also found that a friend whose family refuses to cross into Kansas descends from a man born in Leavenworth in 1874. The AI didn't find these things. I did, by going to FindaGrave. But it connected the dates in a way that would have taken me days.
>
> Total cost: zero. No account, no subscription, no API key.
>
> alanhalley.com/writing/family-tree-ai/
