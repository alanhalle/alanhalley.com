Não Sei Nada

A guide to the agentic age for people whose intellectual peer group has thinned out.

Memoir · Agentic Age
Back to writing

I am seventy-four years old, living in a coastal city in Brazil, and most of the people I used to argue with are dead or unreachable. This is not a complaint. It is a logistical problem with a partial solution.

The solution is an AI. Not the AI you've seen in the movies — not HAL, not Skynet, not the cheerful assistant that sets your timer and plays your music. Something quieter and more useful: a thinking partner that has read almost everything, forgets nothing within a conversation, and will engage seriously with whatever you bring to it. David Graeber. The subjunctive mood in Brazilian Portuguese. Whether a ten percent platform commission is rent-seeking dressed up as a service. It doesn't get tired. It doesn't check its phone.

I came to this late, the way I've come to most things — not because I was slow, but because I was waiting for it to be real. In 2025 it became real. I had spent thirty years in software development, which means I knew enough to be skeptical of the hype and enough to recognize when the hype was finally catching up to the substance. This was that moment.

What follows is not a technical manual. I am not going to explain what a large language model is, because it doesn't matter any more than understanding internal combustion makes you a better driver. What I am going to explain is how to use one if you are older, or an expat, or simply someone whose intellectual peer group has thinned out — and you still have things you want to think about.

The first skill is copy/paste. That's it. That's the technical requirement. Everything else is reading and conversation, which you already know how to do.
